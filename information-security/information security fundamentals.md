# Information Security Fundamentals

## Overview
Information Security (InfoSec) is about protecting systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, misuse, or damage. It involves identifying risks, preventing attacks, detecting threats, responding to incidents, and continuously improving security measures.

## Key Definitions

### Risk
Risk is the possibility of a harmful event occurring that could negatively affect an organization or system.  
In simple terms: Risk = what could go wrong + how bad it would be

### Threat
A threat is any potential cause of harm to a system or organization.  
Examples:  
- Hackers trying to gain unauthorized access  
- Malware or ransomware  
- Insider misuse  
- Natural disasters disrupting systems  

### Vulnerability
A vulnerability is a weakness in a system that can be exploited by a threat.  
Examples:  
- Weak passwords  
- Outdated software  
- Misconfigured systems  
- Poor access control  

## Relationship Between Risk, Threat, and Vulnerability
- A threat takes advantage of a vulnerability  
- This creates a risk  
- If the risk materializes, it becomes a security incident  

## Processes in Information Security

Information Security follows a continuous lifecycle made up of these stages:

1. **Risk Assessment** – Identify and analyze potential risks to systems and data  
2. **Security Planning** – Design strategies and policies to reduce or eliminate risks  
3. **Implementation of Security Controls** – Apply measures such as firewalls, authentication, and encryption  
4. **Monitoring and Detection** – Continuously observe systems to spot suspicious activity  
5. **Incident Response** – Take immediate action when a security incident occurs  
6. **Disaster Recovery** – Restore systems and data after major failures or attacks  
7. **Continuous Improvement** – Review past incidents and strengthen defenses  

## Summary of the Security Lifecycle
Security is not a one-time task. It is a continuous cycle:  

Identify what can go wrong → Prevent attacks → Detect attacks → Respond to incidents → Recover systems → Improve defenses  

## Purposes of Information Security
- Protect sensitive data from unauthorized access  
- Ensure business continuity  
- Maintain regulatory compliance  
- Preserve brand reputation  
- Safeguard intellectual property  
- Enable secure digital transformation  

## Common Information Security Tools
- **Firewall** – Controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on rules  
- **IDS / IPS** – IDS detects suspicious activity, IPS blocks malicious activity  
- **SIEM Systems** – Collect and analyze logs to detect threats  
- **Vulnerability Scanners** – Identify weaknesses in systems and networks  
- **Penetration Testing Tools** – Simulate attacks to find exploitable vulnerabilities  
- **Encryption Tools** – Protect data by making it unreadable without keys  
- **Access Control Systems** – Manage authentication and authorization  
- **Security Awareness Training Platforms** – Teach users to avoid threats like phishing  

## Key Takeaway
Information Security is a structured process of managing risk through prevention, detection, response, and continuous improvement.
